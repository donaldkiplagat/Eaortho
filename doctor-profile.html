Dr. Njalalle Baraza

MBChB (England), MRCS (Edinburgh), FRCS (Tr & Orth), CCT (UK)

Dr. Njalalle is a certified Orthopaedic.

After completing his ‘O’ levels in Strathmore and ‘A’ levels in Brookhouse School Kenya, Mr Baraza studied medicine at the University of Sheffield in the UK graduating with an MBChB.

He did his internship years in the Sheffield and South Yorkshire area before being offered specialty training in surgery and orthopaedics in the Birmingham, Coventry and Warwickshire rotation, culminating in successful completion of the Fellowship of the Royal College of Surgeons (FRCS) exams in Trauma and Orthopaedics in 2016.

education

The following year he attained his certificate of completion of surgical training (CCT) and was accepted on to the competitive arthroscopic (sports) fellowship programme at the University of British Colombia in Vancouver, Canada, directed by Dr Jordan Leith and Dr William Regan, specialising in minimally invasive (key hole) surgery of the knee, shoulder and elbow.

Experience

During his orthopaedic placements he gained plenty of exposure to primary hip, knee and shoulder arthroplasty as well as general trauma. In Vancouver he worked with a range of sports teams including the UBC Thunderbirds (Canadian Football), Vancouver Canadians (baseball), Vancouver Canucks (ice hockey) and Vancouver Whitecaps (soccer).

Achievement

He maintains an interest in academic work, teaching, quality assurance and clinical governance, and has been widely published in several peer reviewed journals.

Special expertise

Dr. Mordicai Atinga

MBChB (England), BMedSci (Eng), FRCS (Tr & Orth), CCT (UK)

Dr. Mordicai Atinga is a certified Orthopaedic.

After completing his ‘O’ levels in Strathmore and ‘A’ levels in Brookhouse School Kenya, Mr Baraza studied medicine at the University of Sheffield in the UK graduating with an MBChB. He did his internship years in the Sheffield and South Yorkshire area before being offered specialty training in surgery and orthopaedics in the Birmingham, Coventry and Warwickshire rotation, culminating in successful completion of the Fellowship of the Royal College of Surgeons (FRCS) exams in Trauma and Orthopaedics in 2016.

Mordicai attended Strathmore and then St Mary’s School in Nairobi, completing his International Baccalaureate. He studied medicine at the University of Sheffield medical school in the UK where he intercalated his studies in medicine with a research degree graduating with an MBChB and BMedSci in 2003.

education

He successfully sat the intercollegiate speciality training exam in 2015 becoming a Fellow of the Royal College of Surgeons of England (FRCS Tr &Orth. Eng.).

Experience

He was subsequently selected to the trauma and orthopaedic specialist training scheme in South West London. During his specialist training he gained experience in trauma management and orthopaedic elective surgery.

Achievement

After attaining the Certificate of Completion of Training (CCT) the following year, he then underwent subspeciality training in shoulder and knee reconstruction and sports surgery. He was awarded a shoulder and elbow fellowship in Waikato Hospital, New Zealand for a period of 9 months supervised by Mr Thin Hong.

Special expertise

He had a further one-year fellowship in Australia at the Perth Orthopaedic Sports Medicine Centre (POSMC) specialising in joint reconstruction and surgical treatment of sports injuries primarily of the knee and shoulder, managing injured members of 2 major Western Australian Football League teams.

Personal Interests

Mordicai remains a keen sports enthusiast. In his youth he was selected for the Kenya under 21 rugby first XV. He is an active club cyclist pursuing both road cycling and mountain biking disciplines. He continues to work hard on his golf swing.
