Mr Njalalle Baraza

MBChB (England), MRCS (Edinburgh), FRCS (Tr & Orth), CCT (UK)

Mr Njalalle Baraza is a certified Orthopaedic Surgeon.

education

After completing his ‘O’ levels in Strathmore and ‘A’ levels in Brookhouse School Kenya, Mr Baraza studied medicine at the University of Sheffield in the UK graduating with an MBChB.

Experience

He did his internship years in the Sheffield and South Yorkshire area before being offered specialty training in surgery and orthopaedics in the Birmingham, Coventry and Warwickshire rotation, culminating in successful completion of the Fellowship of the Royal College of Surgeons (FRCS) exams, Trauma and Orthopaedics, in 2016.

The following year he attained his certificate of completion of surgical training (CCT) and was accepted on to the competitive arthroscopic (sports) fellowship programme at the University of British Colombia in Vancouver, Canada, directed by Dr Jordan Leith and Dr William Regan, specialising in minimally invasive (key hole) surgery of the knee, shoulder and elbow. During his orthopaedic placements he gained plenty of exposure to primary hip, knee and shoulder arthroplasty as well as general trauma. In Vancouver he worked with a range of sports teams including the UBC Thunderbirds (Canadian Football), Vancouver Canadians (baseball), Vancouver Canucks (ice hockey) and Vancouver Whitecaps (soccer).

Professional Affiliations

  • Fellow of the Royal College of Surgeons (England).
  • Arthroscopic Association of North America

Special Interest

After fellowship he took up a consultant post as an Assistant Professor at the Aga Khan University Hospital specialising in knee and shoulder arthroscopy (key hole surgery), hip and knee arthroplasty, and musculoskeletal trauma surgery. He maintains an interest in academic work, teaching, quality assurance and clinical governance, and has been widely published in several peer reviewed journals. s

Personal Interests

When not involved in orthopaedics he engages in creative writing, music (piano and violin), mountain biking, skiing, scuba diving and is currently learning Spanish... whilst re-learning Swahili! He has recently given up motorcycling.

Mr Mordicai Atinga

MBChB (Sheffield, England), BMedSci (Eng), FRCS (Tr & Orth), CCT (UK)

Mr Mordicai Atinga is a certified Orthopaedic Surgeon.

education

Mordicai attended Strathmore School and then St Mary’s School in Nairobi, obtaining his International Baccalaureate diploma. He studied medicine at the University of Sheffield medical school in the UK where he intercalated his studies in medicine with a research degree graduating with an MBChB and BMedSci in 2003.

Experience

He completed his internship year and basic surgical training rotation in The Sheffield Teaching Hospitals training program, gaining membership to the royal college of surgeons (MRCS)

He was subsequently selected to the trauma and orthopaedic specialist training scheme in the South West London rotation. During his specialist training he gained experience in trauma management and orthopaedic elective surgery. He successfully sat the intercollegiate speciality training exam in 2015 becoming a Fellow of the Royal College of Surgeons of England (FRCS Tr &Orth. Eng.). He attained the Certificate of Completion of Training (CCT) the following year.

Professional Affiliations

  • Fellow of the Royal College of Surgeons (England).
  • Member of the American Academy of Orthopaedic Surgeons.

Special Interest

Mordicai underwent subspeciality training in shoulder and knee reconstruction and sports surgery. This included arthroscopy, joint replacement and ligament and tendon repair and reconstruction. He was awarded a shoulder and elbow fellowship at the Waikato Hospital, New Zealand for a period of 9 months supervised by Mr Thin Hong. He followed this with a one-year fellowship in Australia at the Perth Orthopaedic Sports Medicine Centre (POSMC) specialising in joint reconstruction and surgical treatment of sports injuries primarily of the knee and shoulder. He helped manage injured members of 2 major Western Australian Football League teams.

Personal Interests

Mordicai remains a keen sports enthusiast. In his youth he was selected for the Kenya under 21 rugby first XV. He is an active club cyclist pursuing both road cycling and mountain biking disciplines. He continues to work hard on his golf swing.

We are fellowship trained orthopaedic and arthroscopy surgeons looking to inform and empower patients in the East African region and beyond. We are also passionate about East African sports and returning patients to optimum levels of performance.
